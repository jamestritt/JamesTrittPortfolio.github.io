Food Photography

Project: Food Photography

The concept of these images is to envoke an physical and emitonal response of hunger while highlighting and displaying how I precived and enjoy food.

Artist Statement

Food has a variety of flavors, texture, smells, and hard and softness. The combination of these ingredients, make a satisfying experience. In food photography, the photograph highlights the ingredients or product for the viewer. What draws me to food photography is that most people eat with their tasted buds and sense of smell. When I eat, I eat in a different way that most people do. I eat with my eyes. I eat this why because I have a limited flavor palette.

The way I eat is by the visual appearance and description of food. If the visual appearance is dull and boring, like beef stew, then I would over look the stew and pick a more appealing dish to eat. As a person who cannot taste individual ingredients, like in a stew, I can taste sweet, sour, salty, and texture. These photographs of food are some meals that most people can recognize and associate the flavors. I choose these photographs because they have the flavor profile of being sweet, sour, salty, and different textures that I can recognize.

The reasons why I choose to photograph different types of food is a reflection of what kind of food we eat through out our lives. Each photograph shows different flavors we come across when eating. All of these photographs of food share the same flavors of sweet, sour, salty, and different textures. Overall, these photographs show the viewer what kind of flavors they would experience while showing how visual what I choose to eat through texture, color, sweet, sour, and salty.

In all, these photographs are viewed as visual representation of the foods and ingredients that I find satisfying thorough eating with my eyes and mouth. The presentations of these photographs are to reflect my limited ability of taste food and what I choose to eat based of my limited flavor platte.
